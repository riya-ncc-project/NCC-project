Chapter 1 — NCC & Indian Navy Introduction
NCC Navy Wing

The NCC Naval Wing trains cadets in seamanship, navigation and naval discipline. It is administered by the Indian Navy and provides cadets hands-on training in water activities and maritime skills.

Chapter 2 — Seamanship
Knots & Their Uses
KnotUse
Reef KnotJoining two ropes of same thickness
BowlineMaking a fixed loop at end of rope — most useful knot
Clove HitchFastening rope to a spar or post quickly
Round Turn & Two Half HitchesSecuring rope to a ring or post securely
Sheet BendJoining two ropes of different thickness
Figure of EightStopper knot — prevents rope slipping through block
Rolling HitchAttaching a rope to another under strain
Parts of a Rope
  • Bight — curved part of a rope between two ends
  • Standing Part — part of rope not used in making knot
  • Working End — free end used to make the knot
  • Loop — circular shape formed in rope
Remember: The Bowline is called the "King of Knots" — it creates a fixed loop that does not slip or jam.

Types of Navigation
  • Dead Reckoning — calculating position using last known position + speed + direction + time
  • Celestial Navigation — using stars, sun, moon to determine position
  • Coastal Navigation — using landmarks and charts
  • Electronic Navigation — using GPS, RADAR, SONAR

Chapter 5 — Boat Pulling
Parts of a Pulling Boat
  • Thwart — seat for rowers
  • Rowlock (Crutch) — U-shaped fitting for oar
  • Oar — rowing implement (blade + shaft + handle)
  • Tiller — lever to control rudder
  • Cox (Coxswain) — person who steers and gives commands
  • Stroke — rower sitting nearest to cox, sets the pace
  • Bow — rower at front of boat
Boat Pulling Commands
CommandAction
Out OarsPlace oars in rowlocks
Give Way TogetherStart rowing together
Easy AllStop rowing — hold oars horizontal
OarsHold oars horizontal over water
Boat OarsBring oars into boat
Back WaterRow backward to slow or reverse
Stand By to Fend OffPrepare to push away from obstacle
Safety: All cadets must wear life jackets during boat pulling exercises. Never stand in a boat unless instructed.

Chapter 7 — First Aid
Water Safety & Drowning

For naval cadets, water safety is critical. A drowning person must be rescued promptly.

  • Reach — extend arm, pole, rope from shore
  • Throw — throw life ring, rope, floating object
  • Row — go in a boat — do NOT swim to victim unless trained
  • Go — only trained lifeguards should enter water
Treatment of Near-Drowning
  • Remove victim from water safely
  • Check for breathing — tilt head, lift chin
  • If not breathing — start CPR immediately
  • 30 compressions : 2 rescue breaths
  • Keep victim warm — prevent hypothermia
  • Send for medical help immediately
Important: Never enter water to save a drowning person unless you are a trained lifeguard. You may become a victim yourself.

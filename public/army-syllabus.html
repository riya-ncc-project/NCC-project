Chapter 1 — Introduction to NCC
History of NCC

The National Cadet Corps (NCC) was established on 16 April 1948 under the NCC Act of 1948. It was formed on the recommendation of the Pantulu Committee. NCC is the tri-services organisation comprising the Army, Navy and Air Force.

NCC has its headquarters in New Delhi and is under the Ministry of Defence, Government of India. The motto of NCC is "Unity and Discipline".

Important: NCC Day is celebrated on the 4th Sunday of November every year.
Aim of NCC
  • To develop qualities of character, courage, comradeship, discipline, leadership, secular outlook, spirit of adventure and ideals of selfless service among young citizens.
  • To create a pool of organised, trained and motivated youth with leadership qualities in all walks of life.
  • To provide a suitable environment to motivate youth to take up a career in the armed forces.
NCC Pledge
"We, the cadets of the National Cadet Corps, do solemnly pledge that we shall always uphold the unity of India. We resolve to be disciplined and responsible citizens of our nation. We shall undertake positive community service in the spirit of dedication and we shall endeavour to make our country prosperous and great."

Chapter 2 — Drill
Importance of Drill

Drill is the foundation of military training. It develops discipline, smartness, punctuality and obedience. It teaches cadets to act promptly on orders and builds team spirit.

Paces of March
  • Quick March — 120 paces per minute, 30 inches per pace
  • Slow March — 70 paces per minute, 30 inches per pace
  • Double March — 180 paces per minute
Remember: Always step off with the LEFT foot when marching. The command "Tej Chal" is given when the left foot is on the ground.
Chapter 3 — Weapon Training
.22 Rifle — Parts
  • Butt — shoulder rest part of rifle
  • Barrel — the long metal tube through which bullet travels
  • Trigger — mechanism that fires the weapon
  • Magazine — stores ammunition (holds 10 rounds)
  • Foresight — front aiming component
  • Backsight — rear aiming component
  • Bolt — used to load and eject cartridges
  • Safety Catch — prevents accidental firing
Golden Rules of Weapon Safety
Rule 1: Treat every weapon as if it is loaded.
Rule 2: Never point a weapon at anyone unless you intend to shoot.
Rule 3: Keep finger off the trigger until ready to fire.
Rule 4: Be sure of your target and what is beyond it.

Chapter 4 — Map Reading
What is a Map?

A map is a flat representation of the earth's surface drawn to a certain scale. Maps are used in military operations for planning, navigation and communication of positions.

Types of Maps
  • Topographic Map — shows natural and man-made features
  • Road Map — shows roads, highways and routes
  • Political Map — shows boundaries and capitals
  • Physical Map — shows terrain, rivers, mountains
Map Scale

Compass — Parts & Use
  • North is always 0° or 360°
  • East is 90°, South is 180°, West is 270°
  • Grid bearing — angle measured from Grid North
  • Magnetic bearing — angle measured from Magnetic North
Formula: Grid Bearing = Magnetic Bearing ± Magnetic Variation

Methods of Movement
  • Walking — normal movement, used when no enemy threat
  • Crouching — low movement, reduced profile
  • Crawling (Leopard crawl) — very low, slowest but most concealed
  • Rolling — used to change position without raising body
Camouflage & Concealment

Camouflage is the art of hiding oneself or equipment by blending with surroundings. The 5 S's of camouflage are:

  • Shape — break the outline of body/equipment
  • Shine — avoid reflective surfaces
  • Shadow — stay in shadows, avoid casting shadow
  • Silhouette — never stand against sky
  • Sound — move silently, avoid noise
Chapter 6 — First Aid
Definition

First Aid is the immediate and temporary treatment given to a casualty before proper medical treatment is available. It aims to preserve life, prevent the condition from worsening and promote recovery.

Aims of First Aid (3 P's)
  • Preserve Life — keep the casualty alive
  • Prevent Deterioration — stop condition from getting worse
  • Promote Recovery — help the casualty recover
DR ABC — Emergency Procedure

Recovery Position

Place an unconscious but breathing casualty in the Recovery (Lateral) Position to keep airway clear and prevent choking on vomit.

CPR Ratio: 30 compressions : 2 rescue breaths  |  Rate: 100–120 compressions per minute

Chapter 8 — Leadership
Definition of Leadership

Leadership is the art of influencing and directing people to accomplish the mission. A good leader must have knowledge, willpower, initiative and integrity.

Qualities of a Good Leader
  • Bearing — smart appearance and confident behaviour
  • Courage — physical and moral courage
  • Decisiveness — ability to make quick and correct decisions
  • Dependability — trusted by subordinates and seniors
  • Endurance — physical and mental stamina
  • Enthusiasm — positive energy and motivation
  • Initiative — self-starter, takes action without being told
  • Integrity — honesty and strong moral principles
  • Judgment — good decision making ability
  • Justice — fair treatment to all
  • Knowledge — professional expertise
  • Loyalty — faithful to the nation, unit and comrades
  • Tact — ability to deal with people diplomatically
  • Unselfishness — puts team before self
Remember: The three styles of leadership are — Authoritative, Democratic, and Laissez-faire.

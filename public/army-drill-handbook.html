Chapter 1 — Basic Positions
Savdhan (Attention)
  • Heels together, toes pointing outward at 45°
  • Knees straight but not stiff
  • Body erect, chest forward, shoulders back and level
  • Arms hanging naturally, thumbs along seam of trousers
  • Head up, eyes looking straight ahead
  • Weight evenly distributed on both feet
Vishram (Stand at Ease)
  • From Savdhan: move left foot 12 inches to the left
  • Hands clasped behind back, right hand in left palm
  • Arms straight, fingers pointing downward
  • Weight evenly on both feet
Araam Se (Stand Easy)
  • Relax the body but keep feet in position
  • Can move arms and body but not feet
  • Return to Vishram on command before Savdhan
Order of Commands: Araam Se → Vishram → Savdhan. You cannot go from Araam Se directly to Savdhan.

The Salute

The salute is a mark of respect. It is given to officers, the National Flag, and the National Anthem.

  • Come to Savdhan position first
  • Raise right hand — longest route up
  • Middle finger touches right eyebrow (or hat brim)
  • Elbow in line with shoulder, palm facing front-left
  • Hold for 2–3 seconds, then bring hand down — shortest route
Rule: Junior always salutes first. Hold the salute until it is returned by the officer.
Chapter 3 — Marching
Quick March
  • Speed: 120 paces per minute
  • Length of pace: 30 inches
  • Always step off with LEFT foot on "Chal"
  • Arms swing 6 inches to front, 3 inches to rear
  • Fingers together, thumbs to front
Slow March
  • Speed: 70 paces per minute
  • Used for ceremonial parades and funerals
  • Arms swing slowly and deliberately
Halting from Quick March
  • Command "Thehro" given as RIGHT foot hits ground
  • Take one more pace with left foot
  • Bring right foot to attention position — HALT
Marking Time
  • Marching on the spot — knees raised to 90°
  • Command: "Kadam Tal" or "Mark Time"
  • Used to wait before resuming march
Chapter 4 — Squad Drill
Forming Up

A squad forms up in three ranks. The tallest cadet stands on the right, shortest on the left.

  • Front rank — most experienced cadets
  • Centre rank — intermediate cadets
  • Rear rank — newer cadets
  • Distance between ranks: 30 inches (at ease) / 24 inches (attention)
Sizing (Numbering Off)
  • Command: "Daahine se gin" (Number from the right)
  • Each cadet calls out number from right to left
  • Used to count and arrange cadets by height
Dressing
  • Command: "Dahine dekh — Sau" (Eyes right — dress)
  • Right-hand cadet stands fast as pivot
  • Others align themselves to the right
  • Command "Saavdhan" — all return to attention, eyes front
